# Minnesota House District 39A — Final Report
**Fridley · Spring Lake Park (part) · Columbia Heights (part) · Hilltop · Anoka County**

## Executive Summary
HD 39A is a **safely Democratic, working-class, racially diversifying inner-ring
suburban district** anchored in Fridley. Over the last decade it transformed
from a swing seat into one of the Twin Cities' most reliable DFL wins,
mirroring a near-doubling of its non-White population and the broader suburban
realignment of the Trump era. The 2024 precinct map shows uniform DFL strength
(56–69 % across all 16 precincts) with no Republican-majority territory
anywhere in the district.

## 1. District Context & Caveat
After the 2022 redistricting, HD 39A covers most of Fridley plus slices of
Spring Lake Park, Columbia Heights, and Hilltop. Before 2022 the "39A" label
belonged to an unrelated Washington County (Forest Lake) seat; the
Fridley-area seat was previously numbered **41B** (Rep. Connie Bernardy).
Demographic figures use Fridley city as the closest available geography to the
district (~90 % of district population).

## 2. Demographic Findings (Fridley, ACS 2020–2024)

### Population
- ~30,241 residents (2024), up from 29,590 in 2020 (+2.2 %).
- Median age 34.7 (state 38.7); 24.2 % under 18; 14.7 % over 65.
- 11,928 households; avg size 2.52.
- 18.1 % foreign-born; 91.7 % citizen; major immigrant origins
  Mexico, Somalia, India.

Non-White share **27.9 % (2010) → 45.4 % (2024)**. Diversity index rose from
61.9 % to 63.4 % since 2020. Fridley ranks #10 of MN's 915 places for Black
share of population.

### Income
- Median HH income $77,680; per capita $38,100.
- Family median $94,121; married-couple $105,306.
- Poverty 11.9 % (families 7.6 %); 13.0 % SNAP/public assistance.
- Median home value $302,900; homeownership 63.0 %; median rent $1,326.
- Black household income +50 % real terms 2011–2021 — fastest of any subgroup.

### Education (25+)
- HS or higher 91.1 %; Bachelor's or higher 30.6 % (state ~38 %, metro ~42 %).
- A vocational/working-class credential profile — a key reason the district's
  Dem majority is union-anchored rather than professional-class.

### Employment
- Top sectors: Manufacturing 15.5 %, Health Care 13.7 %, Retail 11.9 %,
  Construction 7.4 %.
- Medtronic Operational HQ is the marquee employer.

## 4. Geographic Findings (Precinct Choropleth)
The district contains **16 precincts**: 10 Fridley + 3 Spring Lake Park +
2 Columbia Heights + 1 Hilltop. Three distinct tiers emerged on the map:

| Tier | DFL share | Example precincts | Common traits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deepest blue** | 68–69 % | Columbia Heights P-8, P-7; Fridley W-1 P-3 | Highest non-White share, most renters, lowest income, southern/river corridor |
| **Mid blue** | 61–65 % | Most of Fridley + Hilltop P-1 | Mixed homeowner/renter, central spine |
| **Lightest blue** | 56–60 % | Spring Lake Park P-2, P-1A, P-1R | More single-family-homeowner, Whiter, older |

**Spatial patterns:**
1. **No GOP-majority precinct exists** in 39A.
2. DFL margin **grows as you move south & west toward the Mississippi River**.
3. **Variance compressed** from ~25-point spread a decade ago to ~13 points
   today — the district is becoming uniformly DFL rather than mosaic.
4. Largest precinct by ballots (Fridley W-2 P-2, 2,077 votes) sits squarely in
   the middle and is the closest to the district average — a useful bellwether.

## 5. Synthesis
The choropleth is essentially a recoloring of the district's demographic
gradient: more non-White + more renter + lower income → deeper blue. That same
gradient drove the 2014–2018 realignment from D+7 to D+26 and continues to push
the margins slowly leftward.

## 6. Outlook
- **Short-term (2026, 2028):** seat remains uncompetitive at the legislative
  level. Expect Koegel-style 60–65 % DFL wins.
- **Watch precincts:** Spring Lake Park P-2 (D +12) and Fridley W-2 P-1
  (D +21) — earliest indicators of any softening.
- **Down-ballot risk:** 2024 nonpartisan local races showed substantially
  different coalitions than the partisan ballot — the median 39A voter is
  moderate-DFL rather than progressive.
